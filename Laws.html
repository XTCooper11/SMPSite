Law 1:

Griefing Retribution Act

Section I — Definition of Griefing

  1. Griefing is defined as the intentional destruction, removal, or alteration of another player’s property, including but not limited to houses, shops, farms, or other constructed structures.
  2. Minor pranks that cause no lasting structural damage (e.g., temporary blocks, decorations, or harmless traps) are not considered griefing.

Section II — Proportional Retribution

  1. If Player A destroys Player B’s property, then Player B has the legal right to destroy an equivalent property belonging to Player A.
  • Example: If one house is destroyed, one house of equal or lesser scale may be destroyed in retaliation.
  • If a shop or store is destroyed, a shop or store of equal or lesser scale may be destroyed in retaliation.

Section III — Lack of Equivalent Property

  1. If the offender (Player A) does not possess property equivalent to that destroyed, the victim (Player B) may:
  • Burn or dismantle the offender’s residential home in lieu of the destroyed property.
  • If the offender still lacks sufficient property to equal the destruction caused, Player B may seize or destroy Player A’s possessions, items, and inventory until the balance of damage is met.

Section IV — Multiple Offenses

  1. If multiple properties are destroyed in one or more griefing acts, the victim has the right to:
  • Destroy or burn a corresponding number of the offender’s properties.
  • If insufficient property exists to compensate, the victim may seize or destroy the offender’s items, chests, and stored possessions until the balance is satisfied.

Section V — Enforcement & Finality

  1. All retaliatory actions taken under this Act are final, lawful, and not subject to dispute once executed.
  2. No other players may intervene to shield an offender’s property or possessions once they are found guilty of griefing.
  3. Players are strongly encouraged to gather proof (screenshots, witnesses) before executing retaliation to avoid false accusations.

Law 2:

Property Claim & Signage Act

Section I — Establishment of Property Claims

  1. A build, structure, or project is not legally owned by any player until a Claim Sign is placed.
  2. A valid Claim Sign must include:
  • The builder’s in-game name.
  • The exact date the sign was placed.
  1. A property claim only becomes official when the Claim Sign is shown to both other players and recorded in the Public Database (see Section II).

Section II — Public Database of Claims

  1. The Public Database shall contain:
  • The player’s name.
  • The date of the claim.
  • A visual record (screenshot or equivalent) clearly showing the Claim Sign attached to the build.
  1. Only entries in the Public Database constitute legal property ownership.

Section III — Sign Protection

  1. No player may intentionally remove, break, or replace another player’s Claim Sign.
  2. Any player who removes or breaks another’s Claim Sign is required to compensate the victim with:
  • one replacement sign, and
  • A quantity of Oak Logs equal to the height × width of the build (in blocks).
  1. Compensation must be paid in full within five (5) real-world days of the violation.

Section IV — Enforcement & Penalties

  1. Failure to deliver compensation within the specified time frame grants the victim the legal right to seize items of equal or greater value from the offender’s possessions.
  2. All disputes regarding measurements, dates, or compensation amounts are resolved by majority vote of the three players.

Law 3:

Amendment & Legislative Procedure Act

Section I — Constitutional Amendments

  1. Any proposal to amend or alter the system of government itself (including this Act) requires a unanimous vote of all members to be enacted.
  2. Amendments may include changes to voting systems, fundamental rights, or enforcement methods.

Section II — Creation of New Laws

  1. The decision to create a new law requires a unanimous vote to initiate a session (all members must agree that a new law is necessary).
  2. Once initiation is approved, the contents, wording, and details of the law shall be determined by a majority vote (2 out of 3 members).
  3. Once passed, the law is binding and enforceable.

Section III — Deletion of Laws

  1. The complete deletion of an existing law requires a unanimous vote of all members.
  2. If unanimity cannot be achieved, the law remains in effect.

Section IV — Clarifications & Disputes

  1. If disagreement arises over whether an issue is an amendment, a new law, or a deletion, the matter is automatically treated as an amendment, requiring unanimity.
  2. All votes must be conducted with all members present, and results are final once declared.

 Section V — Enactment of Laws

  1. Proposals are entered into law after the session is adjourned.

Law 4:

Parliamentary Procedure Act

Section I — Convening of Sessions

  1. A legislative session may only begin upon a unanimous vote of all members.
  2. All members must set their spawn in the City hall bed.

Section II — Recesses

  1. Recesses are granted in 10-minute increments.
  2. Each 10-minute increment of recess requires a majority vote (2 out of 3).
  3. Multiple increments may be decided upon before a recess begins.
  4. Players must return promptly at the conclusion of the recess; failure to return in a timely manner constitutes leaving the session (see Section IV).

Section III— Duration of Sessions

  1. A session does not end until one of the following occurs:
  • A player must leave the world, or
  • The majority voted to leave.
  1. If either conditions apply, the session is adjourned immediately.

Section IV — Leaving a Session

  1. Leaving a session without an approved recess is prohibited.
  2. Any player who departs without recess or fails to return promptly may be lawfully killed by the remaining members until they return or agree to return in a timely manner.

Section V — Legislative Proposals

  1. Members may only propose fully-formed laws, not ideas, suggestions, or general concepts.
    Proposed laws may be     amended collaboratively by all members during discussion prior to the vote.

Law 5:

Presidential System Act

Section I — Office of the President

  1. The position of President shall rotate among all members on a weekly basis.
  2. The rotation order shall be agreed upon unanimously at the time of enactment, and thereafter follow the same cycle unless changed by unanimous vote.
  3. At the start of each rotation, the incoming President assumes full duties and obligations immediately, unless they veto the office under Section II.

Section II — Office Veto & Succession

  1. An incoming President may veto their turn to hold the office for that week.
  2. If a veto is exercised, the presidency passes temporarily to the next player in the rotation order.
  3. The skipped player remains in the rotation and will reassume their rightful turn when it cycles back.
  4. A player may not veto the presidency two consecutive rotations unless approved unanimously by the other members.

Section III — Duties of the President

  1. The President shall act as moderator of congressional sessions, ensuring orderly procedure and adherence to established laws.
  2. The President is responsible for the execution and enforcement of all existing laws, including but not limited to:
  • The Griefing Retribution Act
  • The Property Claim Act
  • The Parliamentary Procedure Act
  • Any other laws in effect at the time.
  1. If the sitting President fails to enforce a law during their term, any future President may enforce that law retroactively.

Section IV — Government Property

  1. Any property designated as government property (e.g., City Hall, legislative chambers, infrastructure) shall be considered under the temporary custodianship of the current President.
  2. The President has the obligation to maintain and protect government property.
  3. Destruction or alteration of government property by a President constitutes a high offense, and future Presidents may prosecute the offender according to existing laws.
  4. Any property or items seized by the President in the name of government use automatically become government property. Such property must be stored in official government storage or facilities and is reserved exclusively for public or governmental purposes, not private use.

Section V — Accountability

  1. A President who fails to carry out their duties, neglects enforcement, misuses government property, or destroys government property may be tried and punished under the laws of the democracy.
  2. Prosecution of a former President may only occur once they are no longer in office.
  3. No President is above the law.

Law 6:

Self-Defense Act

Section I — Definition of Trespassing

  1. Trespassing is defined as the entry of a player into another player’s claimed property (as established under the Property Claim Act) without permission.
  2. Trespassing applies to houses, shops, farms, or any other builds legally registered.
  3. Presence on unclaimed or public land does not constitute trespassing.

Section II — Definition of Hostile Action

  1. A Hostile Action is defined as the intentional striking of another player three (3) or more times with any item, tool, or weapon, regardless of damage inflicted.
  2. Strikes delivered in the course of mutually agreed PvP combat or harmless sparring do not constitute Hostile Action.
  3. Accidental single or double hits (e.g., misclicks, knockback from mobs) do not qualify as Hostile Action unless escalated to three or more consecutive strikes.

Section III — Right to Self-Defense

  1. Any player subjected to trespassing or a Hostile Action has the immediate legal right to defend themselves with any means available, including but not limited to:
  • Lethal force against the offender.
  • Seizure of items dropped by the offender upon death.
  1. Self-defense may only continue for as long as the trespass or Hostile Action persists. Once the offender retreats or ceases the action, the right of self-defense ends.

Section IV — Prosecution & Escalation

  1. If a trespasser or aggressor is killed in lawful self-defense, the act is not considered griefing or murder.
  2. If an aggressor returns and repeats the trespass or Hostile Action, the victim may bring charges before the President to initiate further penalties under existing laws.
  3. Any misuse of the self-defense claim (e.g., provoking others, faking trespass, or intentionally baiting strikes) is considered a violation and may be prosecuted as griefing.

Law 7:

City Hall Floor Procedure Act

Section I — Exclusive Right to the Stage

  1. A member may only propose a law while standing on the designated stage in the City Hall.
  2. Only the proposing member may occupy the stage during their proposal.

Section II — Removal of Non-Proposers

  1. Any member who enters the stage area without the right to be there shall be immediately removed.
  2. Removal may be carried out by force, including lethal force if necessary.
  3. A member removed in this way shall be considered ejected from the session until they return and agree to comply with procedure.

Section III — Order of Proposals

  1. Proposals must be made one at a time, in the order determined at the beginning of the session.
  2. A member may not exceed the number of proposals they declared at the start of the session (per the Amendment & Legislative Procedure Act).

Law 8:

Search & Seizure Act

Section I — Judicial Authority

  1. The office of Judge shall be held by the member immediately behind the current President in the rotation order.
  2. The Judge has the sole authority to grant or deny a search warrant.
  3. A warrant must specify the property to be searched and the suspected crime.

Section II — Search Procedure

  1. If a warrant is granted, the Accuser shall perform the search of the Accused’s property.
  2. The Accused must be present to supervise the search at all times.
  3. Any items seized must be placed in Government Property storage until trial or resolution.

Section III — Protections Against Abuse

  1. If the Accuser takes or removes items outside the scope of the warrant, the Accused may immediately resist.
  2. Resistance may include killing the Accuser and reclaiming all items.
  3. Such resistance shall not be considered a violation of the Self-Defense Act.

Section IV — Accountability

  1. Any Judge found guilty of granting warrants without cause may be impeached by majority vote.
    Any Accuser who abuses the search process may be prosecuted by the President or a future president.

Law 9:

Property Boundary & Access Act

Section I — Residential Property

  1. Residential property is defined as any structure marked with a sign reading:
    "Property of <Name> <Date>"
  2. No player may intentionally dig under, build over, or otherwise bypass the border of another player’s residential property.
    Trespassing on residential property is prohibited unless the owner explicitly allows access.

Section II — Business Property

  1. Business property is defined as any structure marked with a sign reading:
    "Business of <Name> <Date>"
  2. Unlike residential property, all players are allowed to enter business property unless the owner blacklists them.
  3. Owners may maintain a list of blacklisted players; these players are prohibited from entering.

Section III — Enforcement & Penalties

  1. Violations of residential property borders may be enforced according to the Griefing Retribution Act.
  2. Violations of business property rules (entering while blacklisted) may be enforced as trespassing under the Self-Defense Act.
  3. Intentional bypassing of borders, including digging under or building over, is considered a severe violation and may result in proportional restitution or other penalties.

Law 10:

Government Property Construction & Removal Act

Section I — Construction of Government Property

  1. Any player wishing to build government property (e.g., City Hall, storage, infrastructure) must be the President at the time of construction.
  2. The President must receive approval from both other members before construction may begin.
  3. Once built, the structure is designated as government property and enters under the custody of the sitting President.

Section II — Removal of Government Property

  1. Any removal or destruction of government property must also be authorized by the current President with approval from both other members.
  2. Future Presidents may not remove government property without unanimous approval from the other two members.

Section III — Enforcement

  1. Unauthorized construction, destruction, or removal of government property constitutes a high-level offense and may be prosecuted under the Checks & Balances Act and the Griefing Retribution Act.
  2. Any property or items seized in the name of government use remain government property and must be stored in designated facilities.

Law 11:

Congressional Permanency Act

Section I — Permanent Congressmen

  1. The original three founding members (current President, Judge, and third Congressman) are hereby established as the Permanent Congressmen.
  2. The Permanent Congressmen cannot be removed from office by any legislation, vote, or impeachment.
  3. Permanent Congressmen hold their positions for the lifetime of the system.

Section II — Admission of New Congressmen

  1. Additional players may be admitted to Congress through a majority vote of the sitting Congressmen.
  2. Any admitted Congressman may be removed by a majority vote of the sitting Congressmen.
  3. A removed Congressman cannot be voted back into Congress until the Judge grants greenlight approval for their reinstatement eligibility.

Section III — Enforcement

  1. Any attempt to circumvent or undermine this Act is considered null and void.
  2. The Judge is the final arbiter of disputes concerning membership eligibility.

Law 12:

Bill of Rights Act

Section I — Establishment

  1. A new governing document, the Bill of Rights, is hereby established.
  2. The Bill of Rights shall serve as a separate foundational document outlining the core rights and freedoms of all players.

Section II — Amendment Procedure

  1. No clause, amendment, or right may be added to the Bill of Rights unless passed by a unanimous vote of all sitting Congressmen (including Permanent Congressmen).
  2. The Bill of Rights cannot be repealed, removed, or diminished by any other legislation.

Section III — Enforcement

  1. Any legislation or act that conflicts with the Bill of Rights is automatically void and unenforceable.
  2. The Judge shall ensure the integrity and enforcement of the Bill of Rights.
